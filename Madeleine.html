Madeleine L'engle

(1918–2007)

1. Born in a Family filling with artists in 1918.

  • Father:A writer(known for mystery stories)and journalist

  • Mother:A pianist

2. Why did she start writing?

  • At first, she made herself not to be lonely by surrounding herself with stories.

  • Because she read all of books at home, she began to create new stories and poetry of her own. She wrote her first story at five.

  • After her dad died from pneumonia (肺炎) when she was in senior high school, she started thinking of be a professional writer. To express her feelings and emotions.

3. In 1944, she met her husband, actor Hugh Franklin.

  • The transportation of the role made her change her writing style.

  • She considered quitting writing several times because she wanted to focus on her children.

4. How "The Wrinkle in Time" be written.

  • The visual inspiration of the story is from the New Mexico border landscape of the Ute Indian reservation and Arizona’s Painted Desert.

  • Madeleine believed that "Science fantasy is not far from fairy tale."

  • It showcased an expression of Madeleine’s Christian faith—of wanting to believe in a higher power with limitless, indiscriminating love—alongside the importance of never being afraid to ask questions.

  • She met a lot of problem on publishing. She finished the book in 1960, but it wouldn't be published until 1962.
